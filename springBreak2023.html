I want to run experiments that compare trees with losses with trees with trees with parallel mutations.
Specifically, I want to ask the question of whether CASet+GMD will yield lower distances than CASet.
I also want to look more into the real data trees CRC1 and CRC2 to make my own intuitive comparisons
between them. Another thing I want to look into is whether two trees with similar mutation sets but
vastly different heights will be punished heavily by MP3.

After some more investigating, I'm wonder if it is the case that MP3 is bad at handling mutatioon clusters.
After looking at an experiment done in the MP3 paper, it seems that MP3 more severely punishes variances in
tree depth. While punishing differences in tree depth seems to be an essential property of a distance measure
for comparing clonal trees, there remains to be said how severely the punishment should be. One side effect
of harshly punishing these types of trees is that when two trees have similar mutation sets but one is more clustered,
that tree is inherently going to be shorter, which may lead to a very severe difference between the two trees. This might
lead to evaluations that ignore the fact that trees at different resolutions could encode the same time of information.
